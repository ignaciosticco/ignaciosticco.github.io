Research

I finished my PhD in 2023, my thesis was about finding ways to improve crowd evacuations. In the first years, I was committed to improving the Social Force Model of pedestrian dynamics using real-world data. Once the model was calibrated, I explored different architectural conditions in order to reduce the evacuation time and also the pressure exerted on evacuating pedestrians. You can access my PhD thesis (in spanish) via this Link.

I developed my research in the computational physics research group (LAFEC), in the department of physics at the Universidad de Buenos Aires (UBA). My doctoral advisors were Claudio Dorso and Guillermo Frank. During my PhD, I have been working on the projects described below. You can explore a comprehensive list of my published papers by clicking Here.

Vestibule to imporve evacuations

During an emergency evacuation, it is of paramount importance to evacuate the building as fast as possible. In this sense, we explore architectural layouts that include two panel-like obstacles in front of the exit door. We define the "vestibule" as the space between the panels and the exit door. Our numerical simulations indicate that this layout produces an evacuation flow much higher than the "no-vestibule" scenario. Find more about this topic in Sticco et al. 2022. and Sticco et al. 2023. Go to this link for a nice video of the simulations.

Parameter testing and optimization of the Social Force Model

In this research, we analyze a real-life video, recorded at the opening of a store during a Black Friday event, which resembles an emergency evacuation. We performed numerical simulations to recreate this event. The empirical measurements were compared against simulated evacuation corresponding to different sets of parameters currently in use in the literature. We also developed a Genetic Algorithm to calibrate the most relevant parameters of the model. The parameters obtained with this algorithm can replicate the qualitative and quantitative behavior of the empirical measurements (watch the results). You can find more information in Sticco et al. 2021

Parameter exploration of the Social Force Model

I spent the first years of my Ph.D. exploring the role of parameters in the social force model. I contrasted the numerical simulation against empirical data from the literature. I focused my research on studying the parameters associated with the physical forces. These are the friction coefficient (Sticco et al. 2020 a) and the body force coefficient (Sticco et al. 2020 b). The latter is modeled as the elasticity coefficient of a spring (which in the crowd dynamics context represents the stiffness of the human thorax).
