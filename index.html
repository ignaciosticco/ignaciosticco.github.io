Ignacio Sticco

I'm a Ph.D. student at the University of Buenos Aires (Argentina) and a teaching assistant at the UADE university. My field of research is pedestrian dynamics. My approach to the field is from a computational physics perspective. I use numerical models to study crowd evacuations in high-density and high-anxiety conditions. My research interest is computational modeling, artificial intelligence and computer vision.

Research

I'm in the final year of my Ph.D. I'm currently working on solutions to improve crowd evacuations by using a vestibule room. Besides, I'm beginning to perform tracking of human faces within large crowds. The goal is to extract data from real-life scenarios in order to perform better simulations and provide better crowd management recommendations.

I'm part of a computational physics research group (LAFEC) in the department of physics at the UBA. My doctoral advisors are Claudio Dorso and Guillermo Frank. During the last years, I have been working on the projects described below.

Vestibule to imporve evacuations

During an emergency evacuation, it is of paramount importance to evacuate the building as fast as possible. In this sense, we explore architectural layouts that include two panel-like obstacles in front of the exit door. We define the "vestibule" as the space between the panels and the exit door. Our numerical simulations indicate that this layout produces an evacuation flow much higher than the "no-vestibule" scenario. Find more about this topic in Sticco et al. 2022. Go to this link for a nice video of the simulations.

Parameter testing and optimization of the Social Force Model

In this research, we analyze a real-life video, recorded at the opening of a store during a Black Friday event, which resembles an emergency evacuation. We performed numerical simulations to recreate this event. The empirical measurements were compared against simulated evacuation corresponding to different sets of parameters currently in use in the literature. We also developed a Genetic Algorithm to calibrate the most relevant parameters of the model. The parameters obtained with this algorithm can replicate the qualitative and quantitative behavior of the empirical measurements (watch the results). You can find more information in Sticco et al. 2021

Parameter exploration of the Social Force Model

I spent the first years of my Ph.D. exploring the role of parameters in the social force model. I contrasted the numerical simulation against empirical data from the literature. I focused my research on studying the parameters associated with the physical forces. These are the friction coefficient (Sticco et al. 2020 a) and the body force coefficient (Sticco et al. 2020 b). The latter is modeled as the elasticity coefficient of a spring (which in the crowd dynamics context represents the stiffness of the human thorax).
